\textbf{Question}:\\
Show that the points $A\brak{2,-3,4}$,$B\brak{-1,2,1}$ and $C\brak{0,\frac{1}{3},2}$ are collinear
\\ \textbf{Solution: }\\
    \begin{table}[h!]    
      \centering
      \input{tables/table1.tex}
      \caption{}
    \end{table}\\
The rank of a matrix $M$ is less than 3, then the matrix is collinear. 
    \begin{align}
        Rank\brak{M} = 1\label{eq1.1.6.22.1}
    \end{align}
Computing matrix $M$
    \begin{align}
        M = \myvec{-3 & 5 & -3 \\ -2 & \frac{10}{3}& -2}
        \xleftrightarrow[]{R_2 \leftarrow {R_2-\frac{2}{3}R_1}}  \label{eq1.1.6.22.2}
        \myvec{-3 & 5 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0}
    \end{align}
Thus we can conclude that the rank of matrix $M$ is 1 and thus $A,B,C$ are collinear.\\
